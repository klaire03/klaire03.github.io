Avast · CCleaner Free

Upsell Journey
Optimisation

Optimising the antivirus upsell shown during the CCleaner Free update flow — a channel generating $1.2M in monthly revenue.

Background

The project optimised the AVAST Antivirus upsell screens shown during the CCleaner Free update flow — improving offer flow, screen design, acceptance rates, install volume and revenue contribution.

Challenge

Telemetry showed low renewals and limited upgrades to the Pro version, with gaps in the upsell journey hindering conversion.

Opportunity

Understand users' attitudes, pain points and behaviour during the upsell — and find ways to engage them more effectively.

Research questions

  • What attitudes, habits and trust shape how CCleaner Free users respond to the antivirus upsell?
  • Which segments are most likely to ignore or skip the offer, and why?
  • Is weak conversion a desire problem, or a UI, clarity and pricing problem?
  • Which variants of the offer flow drive the strongest acceptance and install volume?

How I worked

01

Hypothesis framework

Make every test a falsifiable bet.

Approach

  • Stakeholders added research questions from behavioural and transactional analysis to a shared sheet
  • Questions labelled by urgency (high / medium / low) and mapped to screens
  • Each rewritten as a clear, testable hypothesis
02

Behavioural clustering

Find who skips the offer.

Approach

  • Segmented users by behaviour from transactional and behavioural data
  • Identified high-risk segments likely to ignore or skip offers

What it showed

  • Pinpointed where to focus optimisation, by segment
03

Survey

Understand habits, attitudes and trust.

Approach

  • Semi-structured feedback from users representative of the CCleaner Free base
  • Compared segments and ran Pearson correlation tests

What it showed

  • Provided baseline behaviour and focus areas for deeper testing
04

A/B & usability testing

Validate hypotheses and find the strongest variant.

Approach

  • Asynchronous usability testing via Usability Hub — 50 users (UK & USA), ages 20–75
  • A/B tests in Optimizely on click-through, dwell time, acceptance and install volume
  • Focus areas: navigation, design, content, branding, attitudes, habits

What it showed

  • UI barriers — the upgrade button wasn't prominent, causing confusion
  • Content clarity — users couldn't see the full value of features, creating mistrust
  • Pricing communication — unclear pricing hindered monetisation

Key insights

The upgrade action wasn't prominent enough — a UI problem, not a desire problem.

Users couldn't read the value of the offer, and mistrust followed.

Unclear pricing was quietly blocking monetisation.

Findings were delivered three ways: one-pager for tech, deck for leadership, full report for depth.
